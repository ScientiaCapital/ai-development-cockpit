## 1. Electrical Project Types

### 1.1 Residential Service Upgrades

#### 100A to 200A Upgrade
**Market Driver**: EV charging, solar, heat pump adoption
**Typical Timeline**: 2-6 weeks (utility coordination dependent)
**Price Range**: $2,700 - $6,500 (standard conditions)

| Scenario | Sq Ft | Labor Hours | Materials | Permits | Total Range |
|----------|-------|-------------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Basic (overhead) | 1,800 | 10-12 | $1,200 | $200 | $2,700-$3,400 |
| Mid-Range | 2,000-2,400 | 14-20 | $1,800 | $350 | $3,800-$5,400 |
| Complex (underground) | Any | 20+ | $3,000+ | $500+ | $7,000-$18,500 |

**Key Components**:
- 200A main panel (20-42 spaces)
- Meter socket upgrade/relocation
- Service entrance conductors
- Grounding electrode system
- 2 1/2" schedule 40 conduit (200A service)

#### 200A to 400A Upgrade
**When Required**: Multi-generational homes, full electrification + EV + solar
**Price Range**: $8,000 - $15,000+
**Complexity**: Often requires utility transformer upgrade

#### Smart Panel Alternative (HEMS)
**Description**: Home Energy Management System for service upgrade avoidance
**Use Case**: When 200A service is sufficient with intelligent load management
**Cost Savings**: Avoids $8,000+ upgrade to 400A
**Technology**: Span Panel, Lumin, Schneider Square D Energy Center

### 1.2 Panel Replacements - Hazardous Panels

#### Federal Pacific Electric (FPE) Stab-Lok
**Manufactured**: 1950-1990
**Hazard**: Breakers fail to trip during fault conditions (fraudulent UL listing - 2005 NJ court ruling)
**Identification**: Red strip on breaker faces, "Stab-Lok" label
**Recommendation**: **FULL REPLACEMENT REQUIRED** (breaker replacement alone insufficient)
**Price Range**: $6,000 - $10,000

#### Zinsco/Sylvania/Magnetrip
**Manufactured**: 1963-1981
**Hazard**: Breakers overheat, melt, and fuse to bus bars
**Identification**: Red/green/blue toggle colors
**Recommendation**: **FULL REPLACEMENT REQUIRED**
**Price Range**: $6,000 - $10,000

#### Challenger Panels
**Issue**: Some models have defective breakers
**Recommendation**: Evaluate on case-by-case basis

#### Pushmatic Panels
**Issue**: Breakers become stiff/stuck over time
**Recommendation**: Replace due to age and obsolete parts

**Insurance Note**: Many insurers will deny or cancel coverage if hazardous panels present.

#### 5 Phases of Electrical Construction

| Phase | Stage | Description | Inspection Required |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Pre-Construction | Blueprints, permits, load calculations | Permit approval |
| 2 | Temporary Power | Temp pole or generator installation | N/A |
| 3 | Underground/Slab | Conduit in wet phase (pre-concrete) | Sometimes |

**Rough-In Timeline Estimate**:
- Solo electrician: ~1 hour per 100 sq ft
- 2-person crew: 2,500 sq ft house in 2 days (16 hours)
- Typical 2,500 sq ft house: 2-story, 4-bed, 3-bath, 20 can lights, 6 paddle fans

### 1.5 EV Charger Installation

| Type | Amperage | Circuit | Outlet | Wire Size | Cost Range |

| NEMA 14-50 Plug-in | 40A | 50A | NEMA 14-50R | #6 AWG Cu | $1,000-$1,500 |

| Hardwired 80A | 80A | 100A | N/A | #2 AWG Cu | $2,000-$3,500 |

**NEC 2023 Requirements**:
- GFCI protection required for garage outlets (adds $100-$250)

- New 240V circuit installation

- Trenching/new conduit runs

**Permit NOT Required For**:

**Installation Workflow**:
1. Site assessment (panel capacity, distance to parking)
2. Proposal with permit costs
3. Customer approval
4. Permit application
5. Material procurement
6. Installation (circuit + charger)
7. Inspection
8. Closeout/warranty registration

### 1.7 Generator Installation

#### Portable Generator with Manual Transfer Switch

**Components**:
- Manual transfer switch (6-10 circuits)
- Inlet box (NEMA L14-30 typical)
- Generator cord

**Price Range**: $800 - $1,500 installed
**Timeline**: 1 day

#### Whole-Home Standby Generator

**Components**:
- Automatic transfer switch (ATS) - 100A or 200A
- Standby generator (8-22 kW typical)
- Fuel line (natural gas or propane)
- Concrete pad or pea gravel base

**Brands**: Generac (market leader), Kohler, Briggs & Stratton, Cummins

| Generator Size | Home Coverage | Installed Cost Range |
|----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| 8-11 kW | Essential circuits | $6,500 - $8,500 |
| 14-17 kW | Most circuits | $8,500 - $12,000 |
| 20-22 kW | Whole home | $10,000 - $16,000 |

**Installation Workflow**:
1. Site assessment (gas availability, distance to panel)
2. Load calculation (sizing)
3. Proposal
4. Contract signing
5. Permit application (electrical + gas)
6. Site preparation (pad pour)
7. Generator placement
8. Gas line installation (by plumber or generator installer)
9. Transfer switch installation
10. Wiring and connection
11. Startup and testing
12. Inspection
13. Customer training
14. Warranty registration

**Timeline**: 2-4 weeks (equipment availability dependent)

**Safety Requirements**:
- NEC requires transfer switch to prevent backfeeding
- Outdoor disconnect for emergency responders
- Proper clearances from structure (per manufacturer)

### 2.3 Surge Protection / Whole-Home Surge

**NEC 2023 Requirement**: Type 1 or Type 2 SPD required for all dwelling units

**Types of SPDs**:
| Type | Location | Protection Level |
|------|----------|------------------|
| Type 1 | Before main disconnect | Lightning/utility surges |
| Type 2 | Load side of main panel | Most common residential |
| Type 3 | Point-of-use | Sensitive electronics |

**Best Practice**: Layered protection (Type 1 + Type 2 + Type 3)

**Installation Requirements**:
- Wire leads < 12 inches (shorter = better)
- Dedicated breaker for Type 2
- 6 AWG copper minimum
- Ground impedance < 5 ohms (IEEE standard)

**Upsell Opportunity**: Every panel upgrade should include whole-home SPD ($150-$350 material + labor)

### 2.5 Pool/Spa Electrical

**Governing Code**: NEC Article 680

**Bonding Requirements**:
- All metallic parts within 5 ft of water
- #8 AWG solid copper (minimum)
- 12" x 12" bonding grid under conductive pool shells
- 4 connection points minimum around perimeter

**GFCI Requirements**:
- All pool/spa circuits
- All receptacles within 20 ft of water (NEC 2023)
- Pump motors, heaters, lights

**Equipment Distance Requirements**:
- Receptacles: 6-20 ft from water's edge
- Switches: 5 ft minimum from water's edge
- Overhead conductors: 22.5 ft above water

**Common Pool Electrical Work**:
- Sub-panel for pool equipment
- Pump circuit (often 240V)
- Heater circuit (gas or electric)
- Lighting (low-voltage underwater)
- Automation control systems

## 4. Compliance Requirements

### 4.1 National Electrical Code (NEC)

**Current Version**: NEC 2023 (many jurisdictions still on 2020 or 2017)

**Key NEC 2023 Changes**:

| Section | Requirement | Impact |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| 230.85(C) | Emergency disconnect for 1&2 family dwellings | Required on all panel replacements |
| 230.67 | Surge protection (SPD) for dwelling services | Type 1 or 2 SPD mandatory |
| 210.8 | Expanded GFCI requirements | Kitchen, laundry, all garage outlets |
| 210.12 | Expanded AFCI requirements | More bedroom/living area circuits |
| 250.68(C) | Ground clamp within 5 ft of water service | More specific location |

**NEC 2023 Adoption Status (as of 2025)**:
Adopted in: Colorado, Georgia, Idaho, Kentucky, Maine, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, Nebraska, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Oregon, South Dakota, Texas, Washington, Wyoming

---

### 4.2 State Electrical Licenses

**License Types**:
| License | Requirements | Can Do |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| Apprentice | Enrolled in program | Work under supervision only |
| Journeyman | 4-5 years (8,000 hours) + exam | Independent work, no business |
| Master | 2+ years as journeyman + exam | Train apprentices, supervise |
| Contractor | Master license + business license | Bid, contract, employ electricians |

**Typical Timeline**: 6-9 years from apprentice to contractor license

**Reciprocity**: Many states have agreements through NERA (National Electrical Reciprocal Alliance)

---

### 4.3 Permit Requirements

**Always Required**:
- Panel upgrades/replacements
- New circuits
- Service changes
- EV charger installation (new circuit)
- Generator installation

**Usually Required**:
- Outlet/switch additions
- Fixture installations (varies by jurisdiction)

**Typically Not Required**:
- Like-for-like replacements (outlet for outlet)
- Light bulb changes
- Plugging into existing outlets

---

### 4.4 OSHA Electrical Safety

**Applicable Standards**:
- 29 CFR 1910 Subpart S (General Industry)
- 29 CFR 1926 Subpart K (Construction)
- NFPA 70E (Electrical Safety in the Workplace)

**2024 OSHA Arc Flash Update** (Key Changes):
- Low-voltage systems (120/208V) CAN cause arc flash injuries
- If not in LOTO/ESWC, treat as energized
- Arc-rated clothing classification updated
- Flammable undergarments prohibited under PPE

**Arc Flash Study Requirements**:
- Required for equipment operating at 50V+
- Must be updated every 5 years
- Commercial/industrial requirement

---

### 4.5 Arc Flash Labeling (Commercial)

**Required Information on Labels**:
- Nominal voltage
- Arc flash boundary
- Available incident energy (cal/cm^2)
- Required PPE category
- Limited/restricted approach boundaries

### 6.2 Time & Material vs Flat Rate

| Model | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|-------|----------|------|------|
| **Time & Material** | Troubleshooting, unknown scope | Covers all situations, no risk | Customer anxiety, clock-watching |
| **Flat Rate** | Standard jobs, repeat work | Customer certainty, faster sales | Risk if job runs long |
| **Hybrid** | Most contractors | Best of both | Requires good price book |

**Flat Rate Examples**:
- Outlet replacement: $150-$250
- Switch replacement: $125-$200
- Ceiling fan install (existing box): $150-$300
- GFCI outlet install: $175-$275
- Breaker replacement: $150-$300

**Hourly Rates** (2025):
- Apprentice: $40-$60/hr
- Journeyman: $60-$100/hr
- Master: $80-$130/hr
- Emergency/After-hours: 1.5x-2x standard
