## 1. HVAC RESIDENTIAL

### System Replacement Payment Terms

**Typical Project Sizes**:
- Basic Systems: $5,000 - $8,000 (smaller homes, basic needs)
- Mid-Range Systems: $8,000 - $10,000 (efficiency/comfort balance)
- High-Efficiency Systems: $10,000+ (top-tier energy savings)

**Payment Timing**:
- **Due on Completion**: Most common for residential work
- **50% Upfront / 50% on Completion**: Common for larger projects
- **Net 15**: Standard commercial terms when offered
- **Financing**: Majority of residential sales use third-party financing

### Financing Programs

**Wells Fargo Home Projects**:
- Dealer fees: 0% to 15.18% (varies by loan type)
- Partnerships: Trane, American Standard, Carrier/Bryant
- Special terms: 5 years (60 months) on approved credit
- APR: 28.99% for new accounts (standard rate)
- 0% APR for 48 months available through dealers

**Synchrony**:
- Elite/Preferred Financing: 0% for up to 60 months (through 12/31/2025)
- Works well for wide range of credit types
- Instant decisioning with quick approvals
- Largest private label credit card provider in US
- Ally Lending (merging): No dealer fees on some plans

**GreenSky**:
- Credit limits: Up to $40,000
- Fixed rates: As low as 3.99%
- "No Interest if Paid in Full" promos (28.99% APR if not paid)
- Mobile-friendly platform
- Fast credit decisions for in-home sales

**General Financing Terms**:
- 0% Interest Financing: 6-18 months (good/excellent credit)
- Standard Installment Loans: 3-10 years, 6.99%-14.99% APR
- Example: $10,000 system at 9.99% over 7 years = $166/month

### OEM Dealer Incentive Structures

**Trane**:
- Dealer tools: Proposal builder, rebate calculator
- Training: IRA tax credit education, application processes
- Current offers: Up to $650 instant rebates, 0% APR for 48 months
- Special financing: 60 months through participating dealers

**Lennox**:
- Annual conference: Lennox LIVE (dealer education)
- Fall 2025: Up to $1,800 factory-direct rebates
- 12 months no payments/no interest (qualified buyers)
- Utility rebate integration

**Carrier**:
- Wells Fargo partnership for financing
- Communicating controls/smart technology focus
- Global leader in building technology

**Federal Tax Credits (All OEMs)**:
- Energy Efficient Home Improvement Tax Credit (25C)
- Available through 12/31/2025
- Stacking allowed (rebates + tax credits) up to project cost

### Seasonal Pricing Adjustments

**Peak Season (Summer/Winter)**:
- 10-30% price premiums during high demand
- Scheduling backlogs, overtime labor costs
- Surge pricing for emergency service
- Higher labor rates due to demand

**Off-Season/Shoulder Season (Spring/Fall)**:
- Best pricing and flexible scheduling
- Lower prices, more contractor availability
- Equipment deals from distributors
- 15-25% savings vs peak season

**Regional Variations**:
- Some companies have consistent year-round pricing
- Others offer seasonal sales in off-season
- Distribution pricing may vary by season

## 2. HVAC COMMERCIAL

### RTU Replacement Milestone Payments

**Typical Project Costs**:
- New RTU on existing roof: $35,000 - $85,000 per unit
- With power upgrades/crane: $95,000+
- 10-20 ton RTU: $35,000 - $85,000
- 25-60 ton RTU or VRF: $110,000 - $350,000

**Project Scope Includes**:
- Design load calculations
- Duct design
- New curb, penetrations
- Gas/electric runs
- Seismic detail
- Controls programming

**Milestone Structure** (Industry Standard):
- Contract Signing: 10-20%
- Equipment Delivery: 25-35%
- Installation Complete: 30-40%
- Commissioning/Final: 10-20%
- Retainage: 5-10% (held 30-90 days)

**Payment Terms**:
- Net 15 to Net 30 common
- Permit fees billed as pass-through
- Equipment upgrades require change order

### Chiller Plant Projects (Multi-Year)

**Chiller Replacement Costs**:
- Central chiller failure: $18,000 - $35,000
- Emergency chiller repair: $45,000 - $120,000 (large facilities)
- 20+ year equipment lifespan with maintenance
- 5-8 year extension with regular care

**Multi-Phase Project Structure**:
- Phase 1: Engineering/Design (10-15%)
- Phase 2: Equipment Procurement (30-40%)
- Phase 3: Installation (30-40%)
- Phase 4: Commissioning (10-15%)
- Phase 5: Warranty Period (5-10% retainage)

### Controls/BAS Upgrade Structures

**Building Automation System (BAS) Costs**:
- Small (<10k sqft): $30,000 - $60,000
- Mid-size (10k-50k sqft): $60,000 - $200,000
- Large (>50k sqft): $200,000 - $500,000+
- Controls integration (existing): $3,000 - $15,000

**Major BAS Vendors**:
- Siemens, Johnson Controls, Honeywell (market leaders)

**Energy Savings ROI**:
- Up to 29% energy cost reduction (PNNL study)
- 30% utility bill reduction (Houston businesses)
- Payback period: 2-5 years

**Payment Structure**:
- Design/Engineering: 20%
- Equipment Procurement: 30%
- Installation: 30%
- Programming/Integration: 10%
- Commissioning: 10%

### Commissioning Fees

**Typical Commissioning Costs**:
- 3-5% of total project cost (new construction)
- 5-8% of project cost (retrofit/complex systems)
- Retro-commissioning: $0.10 - $0.30 per sqft

**Commissioning Scope**:
- Comprehensive commissioning plan
- Testing of all system components
- Control sequences verification
- Alarm function testing
- Documentation and training
- Warranty period follow-up

### Retro-Commissioning Pricing

**Cost Structure**:
- Small buildings: $5,000 - $15,000
- Medium buildings: $15,000 - $50,000
- Large facilities: $50,000 - $150,000+
- Per square foot: $0.10 - $0.30

**ROI Timeline**:
- Payback period: 1-3 years
- Energy savings: 10-20%

## 3. PLUMBING

### Water Heater Replacement Terms

**Residential Water Heater**:
- Standard tank (40-50 gal): $800 - $1,500 installed
- Tankless: $2,000 - $4,500 installed
- Payment: Due on completion (most common)
- Financing available for $1,000+ projects

**Commercial Water Heater**:
- Standard commercial tank: $2,000 - $5,000
- High-capacity/specialty: $5,000 - $15,000+
- Payment terms: Net 15-30
- Large projects: 50% deposit, balance on completion

### Repiping Project Milestones

**Residential Repiping**:
- Whole house copper repipe: $4,000 - $15,000
- PEX repipe: $3,000 - $10,000
- 10-year warranty on workmanship (common)

**Commercial Repiping**:
- Well-capitalized contractors work with standard commercial terms
- Multi-family/commercial: Milestone payments required
- Typical structure:
  - Mobilization: 10%
  - Demolition complete: 20%
  - Rough-in complete: 30%
  - Final install: 30%
  - Inspection/closeout: 10%

### Commercial Plumbing TI Work

**Tenant Improvement Projects**:
- Payment terms: Net 15-30

  - Signed work orders
  - Delivery slips
  - Permits
  - Job photos
- General contractors require paperwork before payment

- Residential/small commercial: $50 - $200 per device

- Complex commercial: $150 - $300 per device

**Annual Service Contracts**:

- Factors affecting price:

  - Test failure (additional work)

**Testing Frequency**:
- Most municipalities require annual testing
- High-volume kitchens: 1-2 weeks
- Medium-volume: 1-3 months

**Non-Compliance Penalties**:
- Average fine: $100,000+
- Water service disconnection
- Legal action

## 4. FIRE PROTECTION

### Sprinkler System Install Milestones

**Commercial Fire Sprinkler Costs**:
- Standard installation: $2 - $10 per sqft
- New construction: $1 - $2 per sqft
- Retrofit existing building: $2 - $7 per sqft
- High-hazard/specialized: $3 - $10 per sqft
- High-rise buildings: $4 - $8 per sqft

**Cost Savings Strategies**:
- New construction integration: 40-60% cheaper than retrofit
- Phased installation: Spread costs over time

**Insurance Benefits**:
- Premium reductions: 10-40%
- Property value increase: 3-5%
- Faster sale times

**Typical Milestone Structure** (Inferred from industry):
- Contract/Design: 15-20%
- Permits/Engineering: 10-15%
- Materials Procurement: 30-35%
- Installation: 25-30%
- Testing/Commissioning: 10-15%
- Final/Retainage: 5-10%

### NFPA 25 Inspection Contracts

**Inspection Frequency Requirements**:
- Weekly: Control valves/gauges (pre-action, deluge, dry)
- Quarterly: Main drain, mechanical alarm devices
- Semi-annually: Vane/pressure switch devices
- Annually: Comprehensive system test
- Every 5 years: High temp/harsh environment testing
- Every 10 years: Dry sprinkler system testing

**Inspection Pricing**:
- Small buildings: $250 - $750 (annual)
- Large facilities: $4,000 - $10,000+ (annual)
- 5-year internal inspection: $1,000 - $10,000 (complexity-based)

**Who Performs Inspections**:
- Weekly/monthly: Trained in-house staff allowed
- All other inspections: Licensed fire protection professional required

**Insurance Impact of Late Inspections**:
- Up to 12 months overdue: 5% penalty
- 12-24 months: 20% penalty
- 24-36 months: 60% penalty
- 36+ months: Rated as "Unsprinklered" (no discount)

### Annual Testing Agreements

**Service Agreement Pricing**:
- Small offices: $250 - $750 (initial certification)
- Large warehouses: $4,000 - $10,000
- Recommended: 4x annual inspections by licensed contractor
- Each component has own inspection frequency

**Contract Terms**:
- Multi-year contracts save 15-20% on fees
- Service contracts include:
  - Regular system inspections
  - Warranty services
  - Equipment maintenance
  - Documentation/reporting

### Deficiency Correction Billing

**Typical Billing Structure**:
- Time & Materials (T&M): Hourly rate + parts
- Fixed price per deficiency type
- Emergency service: Premium rates (1.5-2x normal)

**Common Deficiencies**:
- Sprinkler head replacement
- Valve repairs
- Pressure gauge replacement
- Alarm system repairs

### Fire Alarm Monitoring Fees

**Monthly Monitoring Costs**:
- Standard service: $50 - $100/month
- Premium service: Up to $300/month
- Typical range: $50 - $60/month
- 24/7 central station monitoring
- Emergency dispatch coordination

**Multi-Year Contract Discounts**:
- 1-year contract: Standard rate
- 3-year contract: 10-15% savings
- 5-year contract: 15-20% savings

**Annual Monitoring Costs**:
- Standard properties: $600 - $1,200/year
- Premium/complex: $1,200 - $3,000/year

**Additional Ongoing Costs**:
- Annual inspections (NFPA): $200 - $800
- Maintenance/repairs: $500 - $2,000/year
- Equipment upgrades: Every 10-15 years
- False alarm penalties: $500 - $2,000 per incident
- Battery replacement: ~$30/year

**Insurance Benefits**:
- NFPA-compliant monitored systems: 15-25% premium reduction

## 5. TRADE LICENSING IMPACT

### Permit Fee Pass-Through

**General Principles**:
- Per AIA A201: Permits included in base bid
- Real practice: Often moved to reimbursables
- General contractors charge permit cost + markup (common)
- Average permit cost: $400 - $2,500

**MEP Permit Fee Structure**:
- Plan review fees: Percentage of permit fees per trade
- Electrical plan review: % of electrical permit fee
- Plumbing plan review: % of plumbing permit fee
- Mechanical plan review: % of mechanical permit fee
- Commercial trades: Calculated per project cost for EACH trade

**Tax Treatment**:
- Permit fees = Capital expenditures (not currently deductible)
- Must be capitalized as part of project cost
- Tied to specific construction/improvement project

### Inspection Scheduling

**Inspection Costs**:
- Completion inspection: $25 - $85
- Building inspection permits: $200 - $500
- MEP inspections: Varies by jurisdiction
- Multiple inspections required (rough-in, final)

### Code Compliance Documentation

**Documentation Requirements**:
- As-built drawings
- Manufacturer data sheets
- Test reports (pressure, flow, alarm)
- Certification letters
- Warranty documents

**Costs**:
- Documentation preparation: Typically included in project
- Additional copies/certified prints: $50 - $200
- Third-party testing reports: $500 - $2,000+

### Certificate of Occupancy Milestones

**Final Payment Triggers**:
- Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) required for final payment
- Temporary C of O: Allows occupancy, 5-10% retainage held
- Final C of O: Full retainage release
- Punch list completion required

**Timeline**:
- Final inspection to C of O: 1-4 weeks
- Retainage hold period: 30-90 days after C of O
- Warranty period: 1 year from C of O (standard)

### Bonding Requirements by Trade

**HVAC Bonding**:
- State-specific requirements
- Alabama: $15,000 performance bond
- Oregon: Bond + insurance required for CCB exam
- Pre-license training course required

**Plumbing Bonding**:
- Minnesota: $25,000 mechanical contractor bond (gas/HVAC work)
- No state license required, but bond mandatory
- Bond protects customers from incomplete work

**Fire Protection Bonding**:
- California (C16): Contractor's bond + additional bonding
- Idaho: $2,000 - $30,000 bonds (trade-specific)
- Idaho fire sprinkler: $250,000 general liability minimum
- New York City: Master license + background investigation

**General Contractor Bonding**:
- Washington: $30,000 (general), $15,000 (specialty)
- Idaho: $2,000 - $30,000 (varies by trade)
- Texas: Varies by city/county (separate bonds per jurisdiction)

**Insurance Requirements**:
- General liability: Standard across all trades
- Florida HVAC: $100,000 public liability, $25,000 property damage
- Workers' comp: Required for building trades
- Professional liability: Varies by state

## KEY INSIGHTS FOR COPERNIQ SCHEMA

### FinancialDocument Payment Terms

**Residential Standard Terms**:
- "Due on Completion" (most common)
- "50% Deposit / 50% on Completion" (large projects)
- "Net 15" (when financing not used)
- Financing terms: 6 months - 10 years

**Commercial Standard Terms**:
- "Net 15" - "Net 30" (most common)
- "Net 60" (larger contractors)
- Milestone-based billing (projects >$50k)
- Retainage: 5-10% held 30-90 days

### Milestone Structure Templates

**Small Commercial ($10k - $50k)**:
1. Contract Signing: 20%
2. Materials Procurement: 30%
3. Installation Complete: 40%
4. Final/Commissioning: 10%

**Large Commercial ($50k - $500k)**:
1. Contract/Mobilization: 10%
2. Engineering/Design: 10%
3. Equipment Procurement: 30%
4. Installation: 30%
5. Commissioning: 10%
6. Retainage: 10%

**Multi-Year Projects ($500k+)**:
1. Design/Engineering: 10-15%
2. Permitting: 5%
3. Phase 1 Equipment: 20-25%
4. Phase 1 Installation: 15-20%
5. Phase 2 Equipment: 15-20%
6. Phase 2 Installation: 10-15%
7. Commissioning: 5-10%
8. Retainage: 5-10%

### Service Agreement Billing Cycles

**Maintenance Contracts**:
- Monthly billing (membership/club): $10 - $300/month
- Annual billing (discount vs monthly): 5-15% savings
- Quarterly billing: Less common
- Per-visit billing: Service-specific pricing

**Monitoring Services**:
- Monthly subscription: $25 - $300/month
- Annual contract required: 1-5 years
- Multi-year discount: 15-20%

### Financing Integration Points

**Third-Party Lenders to Support**:
- Wells Fargo Home Projects
- Synchrony (Elite/Preferred)
- GreenSky
- Ally Lending

**Key Fields Needed**:
- Dealer fee percentage (0% - 16.4%)
- Promotional APR (0% for 6-60 months)
- Standard APR (6.99% - 28.99%)
- Loan term (months)
- Monthly payment amount
- Total financed amount

### Permit/Inspection Cost Tracking

**Pass-Through Costs**:
- Building permit: $400 - $2,500
- Trade permits (each): $100 - $1,000
- Plan review fees: % of permit cost
- Inspection fees: $25 - $500 per inspection

**Cost Allocation**:
- Direct pass-through (no markup)
- Markup 10-20% (contractor admin fee)
- Included in project price (no separate line item)
