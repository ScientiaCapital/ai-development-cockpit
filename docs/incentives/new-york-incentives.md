# New York Electrification Rebates & Incentives for MEP Contractors

**Last Updated:** December 21, 2025
**Status:** Active Programs
**Audience:** MEP Contractors, HVAC Installers, Solar Integrators

---

## Executive Summary

New York State offers comprehensive incentives for electrification and clean energy projects, including heat pumps, solar, battery storage, and building electrification. MEP contractors can leverage these programs to reduce project costs for customers by 30-100% depending on income eligibility and technology type.

**Key Opportunities:**
- **Heat Pumps:** Up to $24,000 for income-eligible households, $5,000+ for market-rate
- **Solar:** $0.20/W MW Block incentives + 30% federal ITC
- **Battery Storage:** $200-$400/kWh upfront + performance incentives
- **NYC Buildings:** Local Law 97 compliance creates massive retrofit market

## 1. NYSERDA Heat Pump Programs

### 1.1 EmPower+ (Income-Eligible)

**Program Overview:**
EmPower+ provides 100% funding for qualifying energy improvements including heat pumps, insulation, air sealing, and electrical upgrades for low- to moderate-income households.

#### Income Tiers

**Tier 1 (60% State Median Income):**
- **Eligibility:** Households earning ≤60% SMI ($76,680 for family of 4 in 2024-2025)
- **Maximum Incentive:** $24,000 per unit (increased from $10,000 with IRA funding)
- **Coverage:** 100% of qualifying improvements
- **Includes:** Heat pumps, HPWHs, insulation, air sealing, electrical upgrades

**Tier 2 (80% Area Median Income):**
- **Eligibility:** Households earning ≤80% AMI ($102,240 for family of 4 in 2024-2025)
- **Maximum Incentive:** $19,000 per unit
- **Coverage:** Varies by improvement type
- **Alternative Qualification:** Participation in utility payment assistance programs

#### Key Features
- No-cost comprehensive home energy assessment
- Personalized upgrade plan
- Can combine with EmPower+ financing
- **Cannot combine with NYS Clean Heat utility incentive**

#### Contractor Requirements
- Must be NYSERDA-qualified EmPower+ contractor
- BPI Building Analyst and Specialist certifications required
- Adequate insurance coverage
- Commitment to BPI job standards

**Source:** [NYSERDA EmPower+ Program](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/EmPower-New-York-Program/Eligibility-Guidelines)

---

### 1.2 Comfort Home (Market-Rate)

**Program Overview:**
Comfort Home serves New Yorkers not eligible for EmPower+ with rebates for weatherization and heat pump installations.

#### Incentive Structure
- **Air Sealing + Insulation Package:** $2,500-$3,000
- **ENERGY STAR Windows:** Additional $2,000
- **Heat Pumps:** Refer to NYS Clean Heat Program (below)

#### Process
1. No-cost consultation and in-home energy assessment
2. Contractor provides estimate for recommended upgrades
3. Customer receives rebate upon completion

#### Contractor Requirements
- Must be NYSERDA-qualified Comfort Home contractor
- For comprehensive services: Must also be Load Reduction contractor + NYS Clean Heat qualified installer
- Can connect customers to qualified heat pump installers

**Source:** [Comfort Home Program](https://myenergy.ny.gov/how-to-upgrade/weatherization/comfort-home-empower/)

---

### 1.3 NYS Clean Heat Program

**Program Overview:**
Statewide collaboration between NYSERDA and electric utilities to incentivize cold climate air source heat pumps (ccASHP), ground source heat pumps (GSHP), and heat pump water heaters (HPWH).

#### Incentive Rates

**Base Incentives:**
- **Central Air-Source and Mini-Split Heat Pumps:** $2,000 flat-rate bonus
- **Community Bonus:** Up to $5,000 additional for designated communities
- **Capacity-Based Calculation:** Varies by Btu/h capacity and baseline incentive rate
- **Project Cost Cap:** 50% of total project costs (equipment + installation)

#### Eligibility Requirements
- Must be NYSEG/RG&E electric customer OR combination natural gas + electric customer
- **Natural gas-only customers NOT eligible**
- Equipment must meet ccASHP specifications (see Section 5)
- Starting January 1, 2026: Residential clean heat rebates available **only for 1-4 unit homes**

#### Special Programs

**Clean Heat for All: Window Heat Pump Demonstration (Announced October 2025):**
- $10 million available
- Market-rate sites: 50% cost coverage, up to $1,500 per PWHP
- LMI sites: 75% cost coverage, up to $2,250 per PWHP

#### Contractor Requirements
- Must complete NYS Clean Heat contractor training
- Manufacturer-sponsored installation and sizing/design training
- EPA 608 Technician certification (mandatory)
- BPI certification recommended

**Important:** Cannot combine NYS Clean Heat utility incentive with EmPower+ or IRA HEAR for the same heat pump.

**Sources:**
- [NYS Clean Heat Program](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/Heat-Pump-Program)
- [Clean Heat Official Site](https://cleanheat.ny.gov/)

## 2. NY-Sun Solar Incentives

### 2.1 MW Block Program Overview

NY-Sun offers declining block incentives that decrease as more solar capacity is installed in each region. Incentives remain available until all megawatt blocks are fully subscribed.

**Three Main Regions:**
1. Con Edison (NYC + Westchester)
2. Long Island
3. Upstate (National Grid, NYSEG, RG&E, Central Hudson, Orange & Rockland)

---

### 2.2 Con Edison Territory

#### Current Block Status (July 2025)
- **Residential MW Blocks:** FULLY ALLOCATED
- **Nonresidential MW Blocks:** OPEN
- **Program Goal:** 450 MW in Con Ed territory, 1 GW total by 2030

#### Incentive Rates

**Residential:**
- **Standard Rate:** $0.20/W nameplate capacity
- **Example:** 7 kW system = $1,400 incentive
- **Affordable Solar Residential:** $0.80/W

**Nonresidential:**
- **Base Rate:** Varies by block (check real-time dashboard)
- **Prevailing Wage Adder:** $0.20/W for projects ≥1 MWac
- **Rooftop Canopy Adder:** $0.20/W (capped at 25 kW per project)
- **Parking Canopy Adder:** $0.20/W

#### Con Ed-Specific Net Metering
- **2025:** Can still choose net metering OR VDER
- **Net Metering Lock-In:** 20 years if interconnected by March 2025
- **VDER Transition:** Phase Two active, mandatory for new projects

**Sources:**
- [Con Edison Dashboard](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/NY-Sun/Contractors/Dashboards-and-incentives/ConEd-Dashboard)
- [NY-Sun Dashboards](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/NY-Sun/Contractors/Dashboards-and-incentives)

---

### 2.3 Upstate Territory

**Utilities Covered:** NYSEG, National Grid, RG&E, Central Hudson, Orange & Rockland

#### Current Block Status
- **Residential:** Block 7 active (85,000+ kW of 101,600 kW installed)
- **Closing Soon:** Program closes once 101,600 kW residential solar installed
- **Nonresidential:** Check real-time dashboard

#### Incentive Rates

**Residential:**
- **Standard Rate:** $0.20/W nameplate capacity
- **Example:** 7 kW system = $1,400 incentive
- **Affordable Solar Residential:** $0.80/W

**Nonresidential:**
- Similar structure to Con Ed with prevailing wage and canopy adders
- Rates vary by utility and block

#### Utility Rate Context (2025)
Recent utility rate increases create stronger solar ROI:
- **NYSEG:** 9.9% increase (May 2025), additional $11.34/month average
- **RG&E:** 9.9% increase (third year), 18% combined prior two years
- **National Grid:** 28% hike over three years (approved April 2025)

### 2.4 Community Solar (CDG)

**Program Overview:**
Community Distributed Generation (CDG) allows customers to subscribe to shared solar projects and receive bill credits without installing panels on their property.

#### Program Structure
- **Compensation:** VDER (Value Stack) tariff since 2019
- **Subscriber Savings:** 8-12% discount on bill credits
- **10+ Subscribers Required:** For Community Adder eligibility
- **Utilities Covered:** Central Hudson, Con Ed, NYSEG, National Grid, O&R, RG&E, PSE&G Long Island

#### Incentives for Developers

**Community Adder:**
- **Total Allocation:** $111 million (extended through 2025)
- **Purpose:** Incentivize projects with 10+ subscribers

**Inclusive Community Solar Adder (ICSA):**
- **Target:** Projects serving LMI households (<80% AMI)
- **Benefit:** Additional incentive layer for affordable CDG

#### VDER Value Stack Components
1. **LBMP:** Locational-Based Marginal Pricing (energy value)
2. **ICAP:** Capacity value
3. **E:** Environmental value
4. **DRV:** Demand Reduction Value (peak period credits)
5. **LSRV:** Locational System Relief Value (grid upgrade deferral)

#### 2025 Updates
- **June 2025:** Utilities must allow up to 3 different savings rates on single project
- **Anchor Customers:** Can exclude more than one from net crediting

**Sources:**
- [Community Solar Policies](https://www.perchenergy.com/blog/industry/new-york-community-solar-policy-legislation-asset-owners-developers)
- [VDER Value Stack](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/NY%20Sun/Contractors/Value%20of%20Distributed%20Energy%20Resources)

## 3. Battery Energy Storage Incentives

### 3.1 Program Overview

New York has committed nearly $2 billion to energy storage incentives targeting 6 GW deployed by 2030. Programs support residential, commercial, and grid-scale projects.

---

### 3.2 Index Storage Credit (ISC)

**Program Overview:**
Grid-scale program providing 15-year capacity payments determined through competitive solicitations.

#### Key Details
- **Project Size:** Minimum 5 MW
- **Total Funding:** $700 million to $1.42 billion
- **Solicitations:** Three annual rounds (2025-2027)
- **Duration Allocation:** 20% for 8-hour batteries, 10% cap for 2-hour systems
- **Market Participation:** Can participate in NYISO energy and ancillary services markets (contracted floor + merchant upside)

#### Competitive Interest
- **Inaugural Solicitation:** 150+ projects submitted, 18+ GW proposed capacity

**Source:** [Index Storage Credit](https://ny-best.org/page/january25stateofcharge)

---

### 3.3 Retail Energy Storage Incentives

**Program Overview:**
Commercial-scale distributed storage up to 5 MW that provides value to customers under utility rates.

#### Incentive Rates

**Standard:**
- **Upfront Incentive:** Up to $200/kWh

**Disadvantaged Communities:**
- **Upfront Incentive:** Up to $400/kWh (double standard rate)

#### Eligibility Requirements
- New distributed energy storage projects up to 5 MW
- Must provide customer value through:
  - Offsetting demand charges
  - Participating in utility demand response programs
  - Receiving VDER tariff compensation

**Source:** [Retail Energy Storage Incentives](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/Energy-Storage-Program/Developers-and-Contractors/Residential-and-Retail-Storage-Incentives)

---

### 3.4 Peak Demand Programs

**Program Overview:**
Load relief programs pay building owners to reduce load during peak demand events using battery storage.

#### Incentive Structure
- **Total Incentive:** $2,500-$3,000/kW of installed capacity
- **Payment Split:** Upfront payment + performance-based compensation
- **Performance Metric:** How well system responds to grid events
- **Dispatch:** Instant response when called (ideal for storage)

#### Con Edison Targeted Programs

**Brooklyn-Queens Demand Management (BQDM):**
- **Target Areas:** High-load neighborhoods (Brooklyn, Queens)
- **Purpose:** Relieve grid stress during peak demand
- **Technology:** BESS preferred for localized grid relief

**Source:** [Peak Demand Storage Incentives](https://info.peakpowerenergy.com/en-us/bess-newyork-energy-incentives)

---

### 3.5 VDER for Storage

**Program Overview:**
Storage projects can participate in VDER tariff for revenue generation.

#### Revenue Components

**Location-Based System Relief Value (LSRV):**
- **Payment Type:** Fixed ($/kW-year)
- **Purpose:** Compensate for deferring distribution system upgrades
- **Predictability:** Long-term contracts available

**Demand Reduction Value (DRV):**
- **Payment Type:** Variable ($/kWh)
- **Timing:** During utility-defined peak windows
- **Optimization:** Requires dispatch during high-value periods

**Source:** [VDER for Storage](https://eticaag.com/navigating-new-yorks-bess-incentive-landscape/)

---

### 3.6 Federal Tax Credit

**All batteries ≥3 kWh eligible for 30% federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC)**

**Deadline:** Systems must be installed by December 31, 2025 to qualify for full 30% credit.

**Source:** [New York Storage Incentives](https://www.energysage.com/local-data/solar-rebates-incentives/ny/)

## 4. NYC Building Electrification & Local Law 97

### 4.1 Local Law 97 Overview

**Purpose:** Reduce GHG emissions from buildings over 25,000 sq ft (representing 2/3 of NYC emissions) by 40% by 2030 and net zero by 2050.

#### Compliance Timeline

**2024:** First emissions limits take effect
**2025:** Enforcement begins, first compliance reports due
**2030:** Stricter emissions limits take effect
**2050:** Net zero emissions target

---

### 4.2 Compliance Requirements

#### Reporting Deadlines
- **First Report Due:** May 1, 2025
- **Grace Period:** Through June 30, 2025 (no financial penalties)
- **Annual Reporting:** Required for all covered buildings

#### Penalties
- **Rate:** $268 per metric ton of CO₂ emissions over applicable threshold
- **Enforcement Start:** 2025
- **Calculation:** Based on annual emissions reports

#### Covered Buildings
- Buildings over 25,000 sq ft
- Multifamily buildings with 3+ units
- Mixed-use buildings meeting size threshold

**Sources:**
- [Local Law 97 Overview](https://accelerator.nyc/ll97)
- [LL97 Enforcement 2025](https://brightcoreenergy.com/local-law-97-in-2025-what-nyc-building-owners-need-to-know/)

---

### 4.3 Local Law 154: Fossil Fuel Phase-Out

**Purpose:** Phase out fossil fuels in new NYC buildings for space heating, service hot water, and certain appliances.

#### Phase-Out Timeline

**January 1, 2024:**
- 1-2 family homes
- All occupancies <7 stories

**December 31, 2024:**
- NYC School Construction Authority projects

**December 31, 2025:**
- Affordable Housing <7 stories

**July 2, 2027:**
- All occupancies (includes service hot water requirements)

**December 31, 2027:**
- Affordable Housing ≥7 stories

#### Covered Systems
- Space heating systems
- Service hot water systems
- Domestic cooking ranges
- Clothes dryers

**Source:** [Local Law 154 Building Electrification](https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/ll154-building-electrification.page)

---

### 4.4 Electrification Strategies for LL97 Compliance

#### Heat Pump Conversion
- **Benefit:** Reduces carbon footprint dramatically
- **Technologies:** Air-source heat pumps, geothermal heat pumps
- **ROI:** Lower operating costs + avoid LL97 penalties
- **Grid Factor:** Electricity carbon coefficient for 2030 is 50% cleaner than 2024

#### Early Electrification Credit
- **Purpose:** Incentivize electrification work before 2030
- **Eligible Systems:** Electric heating, cooling, hot water
- **Application:** Contact NYC Accelerator for guidance

#### No. 4 Fuel Oil Phase-Out
- **Deadline:** Already in effect (LL38)
- **Requirement:** Switch to cleaner fuels or electrify
- **LL97 Interaction:** Dual compliance obligation

**Sources:**
- [LL97 and Electrification](https://www.sierraclub.org/atlantic/nyc/blog/2025/09/nyc-and-building-emissions-local-law-97-takes-hold)
- [Building Code Electrification Shift](https://violationwatch.nyc/2025/11/20/building-electrification-code-violations/)

## 5. Cold Climate Heat Pump Requirements

### 5.1 ccASHP Specifications

**Developed By:** Northeast Energy Efficiency Partnerships (NEEP) + ENERGY STAR

**Purpose:** Identify air source heat pumps best suited for cold climates (IECC Climate Zone 4+)

#### Version 4.0 Performance Criteria (Effective January 1, 2023)

**Heating Seasonal Performance Factor 2 (HSPF2):**
- Ductless systems: ≥8.5
- Ducted systems: ≥8.1

**Coefficient of Performance (COP) at 5°F:**
- Minimum: ≥1.75 (at maximum capacity)

**Heating Capacity at 5°F:**
- Must be ≥70% of rated capacity at 47°F

**Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio 2 (SEER2):**
- Minimum: ≥15.2

**Source:** [NEEP ccASHP Specification v4.0](https://neep.org/cold-climate-air-source-heat-pump-specification-version-40)

## 6. Contractor Certification Requirements

### 6.1 Mandatory Certifications

#### EPA 608 Technician Certification
- **Required For:** All heat pump installation work
- **Purpose:** Refrigerant handling and environmental safety
- **Types:** Type I (small appliances), Type II (high-pressure), Type III (low-pressure), Universal (all)
- **Recommendation:** Universal certification for maximum flexibility

**Source:** [EPA 608 Requirements](https://www.energy.gov/scep/wap/cold-climate-air-source-heat-pump-ccashp-technology)

---

### 6.2 BPI Certifications

#### BPI Air Conditioning & Heat Pump Professional
- **Purpose:** Demonstrate commitment to energy efficiency and environmental safety
- **Prerequisites:** EPA 608 Type II or Universal certification
- **Exam:** 100 multiple-choice questions, 2-hour limit, 70% passing score
- **Components:** Online exam + field exam
- **Cost:** $175 for online Certificate of Knowledge exam

**Source:** [BPI AC & Heat Pump Certification](https://www.bpi.org/certificates/cold-climate-air-source-heat-pump-assessor/)

---

#### BPI Cold Climate Air Source Heat Pump Assessor
- **Purpose:** Evaluate residential buildings for ccASHP readiness and retrofit potential
- **Prerequisites:** 1+ year experience in HVAC installation, maintenance, energy auditing, or building performance assessment
- **Cost:** $175 for online exam

**Source:** [BPI ccASHP Assessor](https://www.bpi.org/certificates/cold-climate-air-source-heat-pump-assessor/)

---

#### BPI Building Analyst & Specialist (For EmPower+/Comfort Home)
- **Building Analyst:** Understands building science basics, home assessment procedures
- **Specialist:** Advanced knowledge in specific areas (heating/cooling, envelope, etc.)
- **Requirement:** Companies must have certified staff at BOTH levels for NYSERDA programs

**Source:** [BPI GoldStar Requirements](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/Become-a-NYSERDA-Qualified-Contractor)
