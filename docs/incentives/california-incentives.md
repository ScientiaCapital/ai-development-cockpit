### HEEHRA (Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates)

**Program Type**: Federal IRA-funded, income-qualified rebates

**Rebate Amounts - Single Family**:
- **Low Income** (≤80% AMI): Up to $8,000 for heat pump HVAC
- **Moderate Income** (80-150% AMI): Up to $4,000 for heat pump HVAC

**Rebate Amounts - Multifamily**:
- **Low Income Properties**: Up to 100% of eligible costs, max $14,000 per residential unit
- **Moderate Income Properties**: Up to 50% of eligible costs, max $14,000 per residential unit

**Eligibility**:
- Must replace existing primary heating source
- Applies to standalone homes, townhomes, condos, ADUs, mobile/manufactured homes
- Income verification required through TECH Clean California portal

**Critical Deadlines**:
- December 31, 2025: Installations must be complete to qualify for 25C federal tax credit
- February 28, 2026: Final deadline for project completion and invoice submission

**Funding Source**: Inflation Reduction Act, overseen by U.S. Department of Energy, managed by California Energy Commission

**Official Sources**:

## 2. Battery Storage

### SGIP (Self-Generation Incentive Program)

**Program Status**: Active, step-down structure (incentives decrease as funds are claimed)

**Rebate Tiers**:

| Tier | Rate per kWh | Eligibility |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| General Market (Steps 4-5) | $250/kWh | Large-scale storage capacity |
| General Market (Typical) | $150-$300/kWh | Varies by budget step and performance |
| Equity | $850/kWh | Low-income households (covers ~85% of battery cost) |
| Equity Resiliency | $1,000/kWh | High Fire-Threat Districts (Tier 2/3) OR 2+ PSPS events (covers up to 100% of cost) |

**Equity Resiliency Requirements**:
- Must live in Tier 2 or Tier 3 High Fire-Threat Districts (HFTD), OR
- Have experienced more than two Public Safety Power Shutoffs (PSPS)

**Service Territories**:
- Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E)
- Southern California Edison (SCE)
- San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E)
- SoCalGas (SCG)

**Official Resources**:
- [SGIP Program Metrics Dashboard](https://www.selfgenca.com/home/program_metrics/) (updated nightly)

### NEM 3.0 Battery Pairing Incentives

**Program Start**: April 15, 2023

**Key Changes**:
- Export rate reduced ~75% (from 30¢/kWh to 8¢/kWh average)
- Highly differentiated Time-of-Use (TOU) rates
- Battery storage payback now faster than solar-only installations

**Financial Impact**:
- Solar-only payback: ~10 years under NEM 3.0
- Solar + storage payback: 5-7 years (improved from solar-only)
- NEM 2.0 comparison: 5-6 years (solar-only, grandfathered systems)

**Battery Economics**:
- High-differential TOU plans incentivize storing solar power during day
- Use stored power during peak pricing (4-9pm when grid is congested)
- Greater benefit for customers adding batteries vs. solar-only

**Official Sources**:
- [Solar.com NEM 3.0 Guide](https://www.solar.com/learn/nem-3-0-proposal-and-impacts-for-california-homeowners/)
- [EnergySage NEM 3.0](https://www.energysage.com/blog/net-metering-3-0/)

## 5. Federal Stacking Opportunities

### 25C - Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit

**CRITICAL DEADLINE**: Expires December 31, 2025 for equipment placed into service

**Credit Amount**: 30% of costs, with annual caps

**Annual Limits**:
- General cap: $1,200 per taxpayer per taxable year
- **Heat pumps, biomass stoves, boilers**: Separate $2,000 limit
- **No lifetime limit** (IRA improvement over previous versions)

**Eligible Equipment**:
- Air-source heat pumps
- Heat pump water heaters (HPWHs)
- Qualifying electrical panel upgrades
- Weatherization measures
- Energy audits

**NEW REQUIREMENT (Starting Jan 1, 2025)**:
- Product Identification Number (PIN) required from manufacturer
- Similar to VIN for vehicles
- Must have PIN to claim credit

**Stacking**:
- Can stack with 25D (for solar/battery)
- Can stack with state/local rebates
- Total cannot exceed cost of improvement

**Official Sources**:
- [IRS Energy Efficient Home Improvement FAQ](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/frequently-asked-questions-about-energy-efficient-home-improvements-and-residential-clean-energy-property-credits)
- [Energy Star Federal Tax Credits](https://www.energystar.gov/about/federal-tax-credits)
- [IRS Fact Sheet 2025-01](https://www.irs.gov/pub/taxpros/fs-2025-01.pdf)

---

### 25D - Residential Clean Energy Credit

**Credit Amount**: Uncapped 30% tax credit

**Eligible Systems**:
- Rooftop solar
- **Battery storage** (first time eligible under IRA)
- Geothermal heating
- Electrical panel upgrades (if installed with and enables other eligible system)

**Credit Schedule**:
- 2025: 30% credit
- **EXPIRATION**: December 31, 2025 under current law

**Note**: "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" may eliminate 25D credits starting 2026

**Stacking with 25C**:
- 25C covers efficiency upgrades (heat pumps, insulation)
- 25D covers renewable generation (solar, battery)
- Both can be claimed in same tax year for different equipment

**Official Sources**:
- [IRS Residential Clean Energy Credit FAQ](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/frequently-asked-questions-about-energy-efficient-home-improvements-and-residential-clean-energy-property-credits)
- [EnergizeCT IRA Tax Credit Guide](https://www.energizect.com/government_resources/ira-tax-credit-residential-clean-energy)

### 179D - Energy Efficient Commercial Buildings Deduction

**Program Type**: Federal tax deduction for commercial building owners and designers

**Deduction Amount**: Sliding scale based on energy savings
- Pre-IRA: Fixed $1.80/sq ft
- Post-IRA: $2.50 - $5.00/sq ft (with prevailing wage & apprenticeship)
- **Without labor standards**: $0.50 - $1.00/sq ft

**Eligible Systems** (MEP contractor work):
- Interior lighting
- Heating, Ventilating, and Air Conditioning (HVAC)
- Hot water (service water heating)
- Building envelope

**Project Types**:
- New construction
- Building upgrade projects

**Designer Allocation**:
- Architects and engineering firms can claim for government-owned buildings
- Up to $5.65/sq ft for work on government buildings

**Prevailing Wage & Apprenticeship Requirements**:
- Required for full deduction amounts
- Applies to taxpayer, contractors, AND subcontractors
- Must pay prevailing rates for locality
- Significantly higher deduction if standards met

**Compliance Pathways**:
- **Traditional (Modeling)**: New construction and upgrades
- **Alternative (Measurement)**: Upgrades to buildings placed in service 5+ years ago
- Minimum 25% energy savings required (for property installed 2023+)

**CRITICAL UPDATE - Program Phase-Out**:
- "One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025" (Public Law 119-21) phases out 179D
- **Termination Date**: June 30, 2026
- 179D shall not apply to property where construction begins after June 30, 2026

**Official Sources**:
- [DOE 179D Commercial Buildings Deduction](https://www.energy.gov/eere/buildings/179d-commercial-buildings-energy-efficiency-tax-deduction)
- [IRS 179D Energy Efficient Commercial Buildings](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/energy-efficient-commercial-buildings-deduction)
- [alliantgroup 179D for MEP Contractors](https://www.alliantgroup.com/179d-mep-contractors/)
