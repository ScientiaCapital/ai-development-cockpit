## Executive Summary

Florida's electrification market in 2025 presents unprecedented opportunities for MEP contractors who position heat pumps and battery storage as **hurricane resilience solutions**, not just energy efficiency upgrades. With $346 million in IRA funding approved (pending Governor acceptance), utility rebates ranging from $135-$1,000 per HVAC system, and federal tax credits up to $2,000 per heat pump, the financial case for electrification has never been stronger.

**Key Opportunity**: Stack utility rebates + federal 25C tax credits + IRA HOMES/HEAR rebates for total customer savings of $4,000-$16,000 per project.

**Critical Positioning**: In Florida, **hurricane resilience sells**. Frame electrification projects around backup power, storm hardening, and insurance premium reductions—not just environmental benefits.

**Service Area**: 5.8 million customers across 35 counties (largest in Florida)
**Rebate Structure**: Participating Independent Contractor (PIC) program

- **Amount**: $200 instant rebate per qualifying unit

- Equipment must exceed Florida Building Code requirements

## 3. Solar + Battery Storage Programs

### 3.1 Net Metering Status (2025)

**Current Policy**: Florida maintains **full 1:1 net metering** under 2008 Florida Public Service Commission rules.

#### Who Offers Net Metering
**Investor-Owned Utilities (79% of Floridians)**:
1. Florida Power & Light (FPL)
2. Duke Energy Florida
3. Florida Public Utilities
4. Tampa Electric Company (TECO)

**Several electric cooperatives** also offer 1:1 net metering.

#### How It Works
- **Credit Value**: Full retail rate (same as what utility charges)
- **Monthly Excess**: Credits roll over to next month at full retail rate
- **Annual True-Up**: Leftover credits paid at wholesale rate (~3-4¢/kWh) at year-end
- **Cap**: Cannot get credit for more electricity than consumed from grid in a month

**Important**: TECO customers can install battery storage but **batteries are NOT net metered** (interconnected behind the meter only).

## 5. Federal Stacking Opportunities

### 5.1 25C Tax Credit (Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit)

**Inflation Reduction Act Extension**: Through December 31, 2032

#### Heat Pump Credit
- **Amount**: **30% of cost**, up to **$2,000** maximum
- **Eligibility**: Air-source heat pumps meeting modest efficiency requirements (typically 15+ SEER2 / 8+ HSPF2)
- **Income Limits**: None (available to all taxpaying households)
- **Per-Unit or Per-Home**: Per home per year

**Examples**:
- $8,000 heat pump install → $2,000 credit (30% capped at $2,000)
- $6,000 heat pump install → $1,800 credit (30% uncapped)

#### Additional 25C Credits (Can Stack in Same Year)
- **Heat Pump Water Heater**: 30%, up to $2,000
- **High-Efficiency Central AC**: $600 total (not 30%)
- **Insulation/Air Sealing**: 30%, up to $1,200
- **Electrical Panel Upgrade**: 30%, up to $600
- **Energy Audit**: 30%, up to $150

**Annual Cap**: $3,200 total per year across all 25C improvements

**Contractor Sales Strategy**:
1. "Install heat pump in Year 1 → claim $2,000"
2. "Add heat pump water heater in Year 2 → claim another $2,000"
3. "Bundle electrical panel upgrade ($600) + audit ($150) with heat pump → claim $2,750 in one year"

### 5.2 IRA HOMES & HEAR Programs (Florida Status)

#### Program Overview

**HOMES** (Home Owner Managing Energy Savings):
- Rebates for **whole-home energy savings** (not specific equipment)
- **$2,000-$4,000** based on modeled energy reduction (20-35%+ savings)
- **Doubles to $8,000** for low/moderate-income households

**HEAR** (Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates):
- Equipment-specific rebates for low/moderate-income households (<150% Area Median Income)
- **Heat Pump (Space Heating/Cooling)**: Up to **$8,000**
- **Heat Pump Water Heater**: Up to **$1,750**
- **Electrical Panel Upgrade**: Up to **$4,000**
- **Electrical Wiring**: Up to **$2,500**
- **Total Cap**: **$14,000** per household

**Income Tiers**:
- **<80% AMI**: 100% of project costs covered (up to caps)
- **80-150% AMI**: 50% of project costs covered (up to caps)

**Eligible Claimant**: Home builders and developers (NOT homeowners)

**Certification Process**:
1. Home must be certified by approved ENERGY STAR rater
2. Builder claims credit on tax return (IRS Form 8908)
3. Must be new construction (not renovations)

### 6.2 Becoming a Utility Participating Contractor

#### FPL Participating Independent Contractor (PIC)

**Requirements**:
1. Valid Florida HVAC contractor license (A/C A, B, or Mechanical)
2. General liability + workers' comp insurance (per state requirements)
3. Sign **PIC Agreement** with FPL (independent from customer contracts)
4. Comply with [FPL Program Standards](https://www.fpl.com/content/dam/fplgp/us/en/save/pdf/residential-air-conditioning-program-standards.pdf)

**Responsibilities**:
- Install equipment per FPL specifications (AHRI certification, proper sizing, duct sealing)
- Submit rebate applications with:
  - House number photo
  - AHRI certificate number
  - Model/serial numbers
  - Customer account number
- Apply rebate as **instant credit** on customer invoice
- Maintain records for FPL audits

**Benefits**:
- Access to [FPL contractor portal](https://partner.fpl.com/contractor/become-a-pic.html)
- Free training on rebate programs
- Customer referrals via FPL PIC directory

**Consequences of Non-Compliance**:
- Rebate denial
- Removal from PIC program
- Potential fines

**Sources**:
- [Becoming a Participating Independent Contractor](https://partner.fpl.com/contractor/become-a-pic.html)
- [FPL Participating Independent Contractor Center](https://infpl.fpl.com/partner/contractor.html)

#### Common Rebate Denials (and How to Avoid)

Plus, if your AC dies during a heatwave, you're looking at a 2-week wait for installation because every contractor in Tampa is slammed. Let's lock in today's pricing and incentives."
