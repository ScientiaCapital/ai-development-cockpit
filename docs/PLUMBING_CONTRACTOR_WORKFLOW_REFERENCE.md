## 1. Plumbing Project Types

### 1.1 Water Heater Services

| Project Type | Description | Typical Duration | Permit Required |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Tank WH Replacement** | Standard gas/electric tank replacement | 2-3 hours | Usually yes |
| **Tankless Conversion** | Tank to tankless upgrade | 4-8 hours | Yes |
| **Heat Pump WH Install** | Hybrid/heat pump water heater | 4-6 hours | Yes |
| **WH Repair** | T&P valve, thermostat, element | 1-2 hours | Rarely |
| **WH Maintenance** | Flush, anode inspection, PRV test | 30-60 min | No |

**Key Considerations:**
- Tank WH: 30-80 gallon capacity, 40,000-50,000 BTU (gas), 4,500-5,500W (electric)
- Tankless Gas: Requires 3/4" gas line (up from 1/2"), Category III stainless venting
- Tankless Electric: May require 200 AMP service upgrade
- Heat Pump: Minimum 450-700 cu ft air space, 40-120F ambient temp range

### 1.2 Repiping Projects

| Project Type | Description | Typical Duration | Permit Required |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Whole-Home Repipe (PEX)** | Full copper/galvanized to PEX | 3-5 days | Yes |
| **Whole-Home Repipe (Copper)** | Full repipe with copper | 5-10 days | Yes |
| **Partial Repipe** | Section replacement | 1-3 days | Usually yes |
| **Water Line Replacement** | Main water line to home | 1-2 days | Yes |

**Material Comparison:**

| Material | Lifespan | Cost | Installation | Best For |
|----------|----------|------|--------------|----------|
| PEX | 40-50 years | Lower | Faster, fewer connections | Most residential |
| Copper Type L | 50-100 years | Higher | Soldering required | Underground, premium |
| Copper Type M | 50+ years | Medium | Soldering required | Interior above-ground |

### 1.3 Sewer/Drain Services

| Project Type | Description | Typical Duration | Permit Required |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Sewer Line Replacement (Traditional)** | Open-trench excavation | 2-5 days | Yes |
| **Sewer Line Replacement (Trenchless)** | Pipe bursting/CIPP lining | 1-2 days | Yes |
| **Sewer Repair (Spot)** | Targeted section repair | 4-8 hours | Sometimes |
| **Drain Cleaning (Snake)** | Mechanical cable clearing | 30-90 min | No |
| **Hydro Jetting** | High-pressure water cleaning | 1-2 hours | No |
| **Camera Inspection** | Video sewer scoping | 30-60 min | No |

**Trenchless Methods:**
- **Pipe Bursting**: New HDPE pipe pulled through existing, bursting old pipe outward
- **CIPP Lining**: Epoxy-coated liner cured in place
- **Static Pull**: Most common method, no shock waves

| **Sink Installation** | Kitchen, bath, or utility sink | 2-4 hours |
| **Garbage Disposal** | Install or replace | 1-2 hours |
| **Dishwasher** | Install or replace | 2-3 hours |

### 1.5 Water Treatment Systems

| Project Type | Description | Typical Duration |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Water Softener Install** | Ion exchange softener | 2-4 hours |
| **Whole-Home Filtration** | Carbon or sediment filter | 2-4 hours |
| **Reverse Osmosis (RO)** | Under-sink RO system | 2-3 hours |
| **UV Sterilization** | UV light treatment | 1-2 hours |
| **Water Softener Service** | Resin bed clean, salt check | 30-60 min |

### 1.6 Gas Line Services

| Project Type | Description | Typical Duration | Permit Required |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Gas Line Extension** | New line to appliance | 2-4 hours | Yes |
| **Gas Line Replacement** | Replace corroded line | 2-6 hours | Yes |
| **Appliance Hookup** | Connect gas appliance | 1-2 hours | Usually |
| **Gas Leak Repair** | Locate and repair leaks | 1-4 hours | Sometimes |

**Gas Pipe Requirements:**
- Shutoff valves required within 6 ft of each appliance
- CSST must be bonded with 6 AWG copper wire
- Underground lines require 12" minimum cover

## 2. Equipment Categories

### 2.1 Water Heaters

#### Tank Water Heaters

| Type | Energy | Capacity | BTU/Wattage | Lifespan |
|------|--------|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Gas Tank** | Natural Gas/LP | 30-80 gal | 30,000-50,000 BTU | 8-12 years |
| **Electric Tank** | Electric | 30-80 gal | 4,500-5,500W | 10-15 years |
| **Power Vent Gas** | Natural Gas/LP | 40-75 gal | 40,000-60,000 BTU | 10-13 years |

#### Tankless Water Heaters

| Type | Energy | Flow Rate | BTU/Wattage | Lifespan |
|------|--------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Gas Tankless** | Natural Gas/LP | 6-12 GPM | 90,000-199,000 BTU | 20+ years |
| **Electric Tankless** | Electric | 2-8 GPM | 12,000-32,000W | 20+ years |
| **Condensing Tankless** | Natural Gas | 9-11 GPM | 180,000-199,000 BTU | 20+ years |

**Gas vs. Electric Tankless:**

| Factor | Gas Tankless | Electric Tankless |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Efficiency | 80-85% | 98-99% |
| Installation Cost | Higher | Lower |
| Operating Cost | 10-15% cheaper | Higher (electricity rates) |
| Flow Rate | Higher (8-12 GPM) | Lower (2-8 GPM) |
| Venting | Required (stainless) | None |
| Gas Line | 3/4" minimum | N/A |
| Electrical | 120V for controls | 200A service may be required |

#### Heat Pump Water Heaters

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Air Space | 450-700 cu ft minimum |
| Floor Space | 3' x 3' minimum |
| Ceiling Height | 7 ft minimum |
| Ambient Temp | 40-120F optimal |
| Electrical | 240V, 30A dedicated circuit |
| Condensate | Drain or pump required |

### 2.2 Pumps

| Type | Application | Flow Rate | Head Pressure |
|------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Sump Pump** | Basement water removal | 30-60 GPM | 10-25 ft |
| **Ejector Pump** | Sewage below sewer line | 40-80 GPM | 15-25 ft |
| **Recirculation Pump** | Hot water circulation | 5-25 GPM | 10-15 ft |
| **Well Pump** | Well water supply | 5-25 GPM | 50-200 ft |
| **Booster Pump** | Low pressure boost | 10-30 GPM | 20-50 ft |

### 2.3 Water Treatment

| Type | Purpose | Maintenance Interval |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| **Water Softener** | Remove hardness (Ca, Mg) | Salt monthly, resin 10-15 years |
| **Carbon Filter** | Remove chlorine, taste, odor | 6-12 months |
| **Sediment Filter** | Remove particles | 3-6 months |
| **RO System** | Remove TDS, contaminants | Filters 6-12 mo, membrane 2-3 yr |
| **UV System** | Kill bacteria, viruses | Bulb annually |

### 2.4 Backflow Preventers

| Type | Hazard Level | Testing | Application |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| **RPZ (Reduced Pressure Zone)** | High hazard | Annual | Commercial, irrigation |
| **DCVA (Double Check Valve)** | Low hazard | Every 3 years | Fire sprinklers, residential |
| **PVB (Pressure Vacuum Breaker)** | Low hazard | Annual | Irrigation |
| **AVB (Atmospheric Vacuum Breaker)** | Low hazard | None | Hose bibbs |

## 3. Project Phases by Type

### 3.1 Water Heater Replacement Workflow

```
Phase 1: Service Call / Assessment
├── Receive customer call (emergency or scheduled)
├── Dispatch technician
├── On-site assessment
│   ├── Current unit inspection (age, condition, energy source)
│   ├── Location evaluation (space, venting, electrical)
│   ├── Water quality check (hardness, sediment)
│   └── Measure existing connections
└── Document findings with photos

Phase 2: Options Presentation
├── Repair vs. Replace decision tree
├── Like-for-like replacement option
├── Upgrade options (tankless, heat pump)
├── Water treatment add-on discussion
└── PRV check and recommendation

Phase 3: Proposal
├── Generate quote with options
├── Include rebate information
├── Financing options
├── Permit fees (if applicable)
├── Timeline expectation
└── Customer signature/approval

Phase 4: Customer Approval
├── Signed proposal/contract
├── Payment terms (deposit, final)
├── Schedule installation date
└── Order equipment (if special order)

Phase 5: Permit (if required)
├── Submit permit application
├── Pay permit fees
├── Receive permit approval
└── Post permit at job site

Phase 6: Installation
├── Arrive on-site, protect work area
├── Turn off water and gas/electric
├── Drain and disconnect old unit
├── Remove old unit
├── Install new unit
│   ├── Set and level
│   ├── Connect water lines (copper/PEX)
│   ├── Connect gas line (if gas) with shutoff
│   ├── Connect electrical (if electric)
│   ├── Install T&P relief valve and drain
│   ├── Install expansion tank (if required)
│   └── Connect venting (if applicable)
├── Fill and pressure test
├── Leak check all connections
├── Set temperature (120F standard)
└── Clean work area

Phase 7: Inspection (if required)
├── Schedule inspection
├── Inspector verifies code compliance
├── Address any corrections
└── Obtain passed inspection

Phase 8: Customer Walkthrough
├── Demonstrate operation
├── Show shut-off locations
├── Explain temperature setting
├── Review maintenance requirements
├── Present warranty information
└── Collect final payment

Phase 9: Closeout
├── Remove old unit (dispose/recycle)
├── Send completion documentation
├── Register warranty with manufacturer
├── Schedule maintenance reminder
└── Request review/referral
```

### 3.2 Whole-Home Repiping Workflow

```
Phase 1: Assessment & Planning
├── Receive inquiry (leak, low pressure, discolored water)
├── Schedule repipe evaluation
├── Video camera inspection (if needed)
├── Whole-home pipe assessment
│   ├── Material identification (galvanized, copper, poly)
│   ├── Pipe condition evaluation
│   ├── Fixture count
│   ├── Access points identification
│   └── Layout documentation
└── Discuss material options (PEX vs. copper)

Phase 2: Proposal & Approval
├── Detailed scope of work
├── Material specifications
├── Timeline (typically 3-5 days for PEX)
├── Price (by fixture count or linear foot)
├── Drywall repair inclusion (yes/no)
├── Payment schedule
└── Customer signature

Phase 3: Permit & Preparation
├── Submit permit application
├── Schedule start date
├── Order materials
├── Pre-job customer meeting
│   ├── Explain daily schedule
│   ├── Identify water shutoff periods
│   └── Furniture/access preparation
└── Coordinate drywall contractor (if needed)

Phase 4: Removal of Old Pipes
├── Protect work areas (plastic sheeting)
├── Shut off main water supply
├── Cut access holes in drywall/ceiling
├── Remove old pipe sections
├── Cap existing connections
└── Haul away old materials

Phase 5: Installation of New Pipes
├── Install main water line
├── Run trunk lines
├── Install manifold (if PEX)
├── Run individual fixture lines
├── Install shut-off valves
├── Install hose bibbs
├── Connect all fixtures
└── Label lines at manifold

Phase 6: Testing & Inspection
├── Pressure test entire system
├── Check all connections for leaks
├── Schedule rough-in inspection
├── Inspector verifies code compliance
├── Address any corrections
└── Pass rough-in inspection

Phase 7: Restoration & Cleanup
├── Coordinate drywall repair
├── Patch access holes
├── Prime and paint patches
├── Clean up all debris
└── Final walkthrough

Phase 8: Final Inspection & Closeout
├── Schedule final inspection (if required)
├── Demonstrate system to customer
├── Show shut-off locations
├── Provide warranty documentation
├── Collect final payment
└── Schedule follow-up
```

### 3.3 Drain Cleaning / Camera Inspection Workflow

```
Phase 1: Service Call
├── Receive call (slow drain, backup, gurgling)
├── Qualify the call
│   ├── Which drain(s) affected?
│   ├── Single or multiple fixtures?
│   ├── Recent history?
│   └── Sewage backup present?
├── Dispatch technician
└── Estimated arrival time

Phase 2: On-Site Assessment
├── Interview customer
├── Verify symptoms
├── Locate cleanouts
├── Initial diagnosis
└── Explain options and pricing

Phase 3: Camera Inspection (if needed)
├── Insert camera through cleanout
├── Navigate to blockage/issue
├── Record video footage
├── Identify problem
│   ├── Root intrusion
│   ├── Grease buildup
│   ├── Bellied pipe
│   ├── Collapsed section
│   └── Foreign object
├── Measure depth and location
└── Show customer on screen

Phase 4: Cleaning Service
├── Select appropriate method
│   ├── Cable/Snake (standard clogs)
│   ├── Hydro Jet (heavy buildup, grease, roots)
│   └── Combination approach
├── Execute cleaning
├── Re-inspect with camera
├── Verify clear flow
└── Document before/after

Phase 5: Recommendations & Closeout
├── Explain findings to customer
├── Recommend repairs if needed
├── Provide estimate for repairs
├── Discuss preventive maintenance
├── Collect payment
└── Provide documentation/video
```

### 3.4 New Construction Plumbing Workflow

```
Phase 1: Underground Rough-In
├── Review blueprints with GC
├── Mark pipe locations
├── Excavate trenches
├── Install DWV under slab
│   ├── Main sewer connection
│   ├── Toilet flanges
│   ├── Shower drains
│   └── Tub drains
├── Install water lines under slab
├── Pressure test (water or air)
├── Schedule underground inspection
├── Pour concrete after passing
└── Accurate measurements critical

Phase 2: Above-Ground Rough-In (Top-Out)
├── Install vertical waste stacks
├── Install horizontal drain branches
├── Install vent pipes through roof
├── Run hot and cold water lines
├── Install stub-outs at fixtures
├── Install washing machine box
├── Install ice maker line
├── Pressure test water (80 PSI)
├── DWV test (water or air)
└── Coordinate with electrical/HVAC

Phase 3: Rough-In Inspection
├── Schedule with building inspector
├── All pipes visible and accessible
├── Verify proper slopes
├── Verify proper support/strapping
├── Verify proper materials
├── Address any corrections
└── Pass inspection before closing walls

Phase 4: Trim-Out / Final
├── Install toilets
├── Install sinks and faucets
├── Install tub/shower trim
├── Install water heater
├── Connect dishwasher
├── Connect garbage disposal
├── Connect washing machine
├── Connect ice maker
├── Install outdoor hose bibbs
└── Test all fixtures

Phase 5: Final Inspection & Turnover
├── Schedule final inspection
├── Run all fixtures for inspector
├── Verify no leaks
├── Address any corrections
├── Pass final inspection
├── Homeowner orientation
├── Warranty documentation
└── Closeout
```

## 4. Compliance Requirements

### 4.1 Plumbing Codes by State

| Code | States |
|------|--------|
| **IPC (International Plumbing Code)** | AL, AR, CO, CT, DE, GA, KS, MS, NE, NH, OK, PA, RI, SC, TN, TX |
| **UPC (Uniform Plumbing Code)** | AK, CA, HI, ID, IA, MO, MT, NM, OR, SD |
| **State-Specific** | KY, LA, NJ (NSPC) |

**Note:** Both IPC and UPC update on 3-year cycles. Current versions: 2024 IPC, 2024 UPC.

### 4.2 License Requirements

| License Type | Requirements |
|--------------|--------------|
| **Apprentice** | Register with state, work under journeyman/master |
| **Journeyman** | 3-4 years apprenticeship, pass exam |
| **Master Plumber** | 2-5 years as journeyman, pass exam |
| **Contractor** | Master license + business license + insurance + bond |

**State Variations:**
- Some states license at state level (TX, GA, AK, MN)
- Some states defer to local jurisdictions (KS, MO)
- California requires C-36 license for jobs over $500

### 4.3 Permit Requirements

| Project Type | Permit Usually Required | Notes |
|--------------|------------------------|-------|
| Water heater replacement | Yes | Most jurisdictions |
| Tankless conversion | Yes | Gas line + venting changes |
| Whole-home repipe | Yes | Always |
| Sewer line replacement | Yes | Always |
| Fixture replacement | Rarely | No permit if like-for-like |
| Drain cleaning | No | Service work |
| Water softener install | Varies | Check local requirements |
| Gas line work | Yes | Always |
| New construction | Yes | Always |

### 4.4 Backflow Prevention Requirements

| Assembly Type | Testing Frequency | Tester Requirements |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| RPZ | Annual | Certified tester |
| DCVA | Every 3 years | Certified tester |
| PVB | Annual | Certified tester |

**Certification:**
- ASSE 5000 Series standard
- Minimum 40-hour training course
- Pass written exam (70% minimum)
- Annual test kit calibration required

### 4.5 Gas Line Compliance

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Shutoff valve location | Within 6 ft of appliance |
| CSST bonding | 6 AWG copper wire |
| Underground cover | 12" minimum |
| Inspections | Pressure test + final |
| Licensed installer | Required in most jurisdictions |

### 5.4 Hydro Jetting Protocol

```
Step 1: Camera Inspection
├── Insert camera through cleanout
├── Document condition and blockage
├── Verify pipe can withstand jetting
└── Identify pipe material and age

Step 2: Equipment Setup
├── Connect to water supply
├── Select appropriate nozzle
│   ├── Forward jet (break up clogs)
│   ├── Rear jet (scrub walls)
│   ├── Combination (roots, grease)
│   └── Penetrator (heavy blockages)
└── Set pressure (1,500-4,000 PSI)

Step 3: Execution
├── Insert hose through cleanout
├── Advance to blockage
├── Activate high-pressure water
├── Work back and forth
├── Pull back slowly while jetting
└── Repeat as needed

Step 4: Verification
├── Final camera inspection
├── Document cleared line
├── Show customer before/after
└── Recommend maintenance schedule
```

- Lower water bills

**Pricing:**
- Standard installation: $500-875
- Complex installation: $1,250-2,100

- Water softener + PRV relocation = natural upsell
