### 5. **Backflow Certification Annual Programs (Compliance-Driven Recurring Revenue)**

#### **Regulatory Requirements:**
- **Mandatory annual testing** to ensure drinking water safety
- Federal and state laws require annual testing by certified backflow tester
- Prevents contaminants, pesticides, chemicals from entering drinking water supply
- Municipal codes mandate testing to ensure no cross-connection

#### **Recurring Revenue Opportunity:**
**Testimonial from Certified Tester:**
"Has helped me expand my plumbing business. If you want another source of income this is a class to take... Every year backflows need to be tested, it's mandatory by the county. So getting new clients is easy."

#### **Services That Generate Recurring Revenue:**
- Annual testing for fire suppression systems, medical facilities, restaurants
- Official certification submission to local water authorities
- Immediate repairs to prevent compliance issues

#### **Certification Requirements for Testers:**
- Minimum 5 years documented practical experience (plumbing, mechanical, fire protection, lawn irrigation, or related field)
- Successful completion of minimum 40-hour training course on backflow assembly testing
- Pass 100-question exam with 70%+ score
- Pass practical exam
- Currently 19,000+ ASSE Certified backflow professionals (all 50 states + Canada)

#### **Compliance Enforcement:**
- If backflow prevention device hasn't been tested/certified, property owners risk water shutoff
- Tests required annually AND after installation, relocation, or repair of backflow-prevention assembly

**Sources:** [CPUC - Registered Demand Response Providers](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/electric-costs/demand-response-dr/registered-demand-response-providers-drps-aggregators-and-faq)

---

#### **Role of Aggregators:**
**DRP/Aggregator:**
- Commercial entity providing demand response programs and services
- Assists retail customers participate in wholesale energy market
- Strategies or technology to reduce electric consumption during grid needs
- Exchange for compensation

**How They Work:**
- Approach and enter contracts with companies in utility service territory or electric region
- Aggregate companies into large block of curtailable load

**Utilities as Aggregators:**
- Utilities often act as aggregators in RTO programs
- Example: ERCOT allows demand response to offer into energy and reserve resource markets
- Deploys utility programs and other DR resources under emergency conditions
- Individual utilities offer unique DR programs
- Can be combined with grid-level programs
- Creates powerful synergy supporting both grid and utility system needs

**Sources:**
- [CPUC - Registered Demand Response Providers](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/electric-costs/demand-response-dr/registered-demand-response-providers-drps-aggregators-and-faq)

#### **Residential Programs:**
- Large electricity consumers present greatest opportunity
- Some states: Residential consumers can participate in programs aggregating individual consumer impact

**Most Common Residential Program:**
- Air conditioning cycling
- During demand response event: AC runs only 20 minutes per hour
- Easily participate using:
  - Smart thermostats
  - Smart plugs
  - Lighting
  - EVs
  - Batteries
  - Other devices
- Save energy and receive payments throughout year
- Enable and incentivize use of internet-connected thermostats, water heaters, EVs, connected products
- Save energy at peak demand times

#### **Commercial Programs:**
**Typical Program Requirements:**
- Very reasonable
- Day's notice of demand response event
- Curtail electricity consumption for 4-6 hours
- Maximum of 12 events per year

**Example Program:**
**Capacity Bidding Program (CBP):**
- Aggregator managed program
- Operates with Day-Ahead option
- Runs May 1 - October 31
- Numerous aggregators participating
