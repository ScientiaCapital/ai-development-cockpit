## 1. Illinois Shines (Adjustable Block Program)

### Overview
Illinois Shines (statutorily defined as the Adjustable Block Program) supports the development of community solar projects and on-site solar (distributed generation). The program provides incentives through payments for Renewable Energy Credits (RECs) generated by participating projects over 15-20 years of operation.

### Program Goals
- **2025-2026 Target:** 1,500,000 RECs delivered annually
- **2030-2031 Target:** 2,000,000 RECs delivered annually
- **Expectation:** Program to remain open well into the future

### Distributed Generation (DG) Rebates

#### 2025-26 REC Pricing (per MWh/year)

| System Size | Group A | Group B |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| 0-10 kW | $66.34 | $75.48 |
| >10-25 kW | $57.18 | $69.78 |
| >25-100 kW | $59.53 | $69.65 |
| >100-200 kW | $55.63 | $65.09 |
| >200-500 kW | $45.64 | $53.40 |
| >500-2000 kW | $42.37 | $49.57 |
| >2000-5000 kW | $31.96 | $37.39 |

**Contract Terms:**
- Distributed Generation: 15-year REC contracts
- Public School projects: 20-year REC contracts
- Community-Driven Community Solar: 15-year contracts

**Additional Incentives:**
- **Small DG Adder:** $20/REC for qualifying customer-owned small distributed generation projects (proposed for 2026)
- **Public School Adder:** Extra $5/REC for public school solar development

### Net Metering Changes (Effective January 1, 2025)

**Before January 1, 2025:**
- Full retail rate net metering (1:1) available for residential and small business customers in Ameren, ComEd, or MidAmerican service territories
- Must submit application prior to January 1, 2025
- Cannot take smart inverter or distributed generation rebate

**After January 1, 2025:**
- New enrollments receive supply credits only (lower rate per kWh)
- Customers enrolled before January 1, 2025 maintain full retail net metering for system life (unless material modifications made)

### Community Solar

**2025-26 Capacity Allocation:**
- **Total Community Solar Capacity:** ~435 MW
- **Traditional Community Solar (TCS):**
  - Group A: 64 MW
  - Group B: 149 MW
- **Community-Driven Community Solar**
- **Public School Community Solar**

**Small Subscriber Requirements:**
- At least 50% of facility's nameplate capacity must be subscribed by residential or small commercial customers
- Individual subscriptions: 25 kW or less

**Subscriber Savings:**
- Bill credits equal to supply and transmission portions of retail price (60-80% of total kWh cost)
- Typical advertised savings: 10-20%

### Utility-Specific DG Rebates

**Ameren and ComEd customers** can receive cash rebates by enrolling in the Distributed Generation program, allowing utilities temporary control of solar inverters or batteries.

## 2. ComEd Programs

**Service Territory:** Northern Illinois (Chicago area and surrounding counties)
**Program Website:** comed.com/waystosave

### Heat Pump Rebates (2025)

#### Centrally Ducted Air Source Heat Pumps

| Efficiency Tier | SEER2 | EER2 | HSPF2 | Rebate |
|----------------|-------|------|-------|--------|
| Tier 1 | 15.2 | 9.4 | 8.1 | $1,400 |
| Tier 2 | 17 | 9.4 | 8.5 | $2,000 |

#### Ductless Mini-Split Heat Pumps

| Efficiency Tier | SEER2 | EER2 | HSPF2 | Rebate |
|----------------|-------|------|-------|--------|
| Ductless Mini-Split | 17 | 9.4 | 10 | $1,000 |

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must be installed by a ComEd Residential Trade Ally contractor
- ComEd residential delivery service customer
- Systems must be ENERGY STAR certified cold climate heat pumps

### Smart Thermostat Rebates

- **Rebate Amount:** Up to $75
- **Requirements:**
  - Must be ENERGY STAR certified
  - Purchased, installed, and registered by December 31, 2025
  - Application due within 60 days of purchase or by February 28, 2026 (whichever comes first)

### Application Process

1. Visit **ComEd.com** or call **1-855-433-2700**
2. Upload supporting documentation:
   - ComEd Residential Trade Ally invoice
   - AHRI Number (for central AC or heat pump)
3. Online application available at ComEd rebate portal

### Income-Eligible Programs

**Free Home Energy Assessments:**
- In-home and virtual assessments available
- Personalized recommendations
- No-cost energy-saving products: LED bulbs, advanced power strips, programmable thermostats

**Whole Home Electrification Improvements:**
- Free weatherization services (insulation, air sealing)
- Free advanced power strips and smart thermostats
- HVAC upgrades at no cost
- Health and safety measures included

**Low-Income Discount Program (2025):**
- Percentage-based discount on monthly electric bills
- Tiered by income level:
  - Greatest discount: ≤50% of Federal Poverty Level (FPL)
  - Eligible up to 300% FPL
- Expected launch: Mid-2025 (pending ICC approval)

**Food Pantry Partnership:**
- Free LED light bulbs, weather stripping, and efficient products distributed through food pantries

### Financing Options

Visit ComEd's Financing Options page for information about loans and financing for:
- Residential customers
- Multi-family buildings
- Small business customers

## 4. Climate and Equitable Jobs Act (CEJA)

**Signed into Law:** September 15, 2021 (Public Act 102-0662)
**Goal:** 100% clean energy by 2050, 100% carbon-free power by 2045

### Key Provisions

#### Clean Energy Targets
- **2030:** 40% renewable energy
- **2040:** 50% renewable energy
- **2045:** 100% carbon-free power
- **2050:** 100% clean energy

#### Electric Vehicles
- **Goal:** 1 million EVs on Illinois roads by 2030
- **EV Purchase Rebate:** Up to $4,000 (launched July 2022)
- **Public Transit, School Buses, City Vehicles:** Electrification incentives
- **EV Car-Sharing Programs:** Support for creative programs
- **Lower-Income EV Rebates:** Special incentives

### EV Charging Infrastructure

**Illinois EPA Grants (2025):**
- **Grant Coverage:** Up to 80% of eligible project costs
- **Eligible Equipment:** Level 2 and DC fast chargers
- **Additional Rebates:** For chargers in underserved and environmental justice communities
- **Application Process:** Rolling basis through "Driving a Cleaner Illinois – CEJA EV Charging NOFO"

**Contractor Requirements for EV Charging:**
- Licensed installation contractor required
- Compliance with health and safety codes
- Architectural standards compliance
- Certificate of insurance required

**ComEd EV Rebates (2025):**
- **Total Program:** $100M in rebates
- **Three Programs:**
  1. Residential EV charger and installation costs
  2. All-electric fleet vehicles
  3. Business and public sector make-ready charging infrastructure
- **Equity Focus:** More than 50% of funding to low-income and equity-eligible communities

**Federal Funding:**
- **$114M from USDOT:** 14 truck charging hubs with 345 individual charging ports for heavy-duty freight vehicles

### Beneficial Electrification Plans

**Requirements (Effective July 1, 2022):**
- Electric utilities serving >500,000 customers must file Beneficial Electrification Plan with ICC
- Funding provided for public and private organizations to install and maintain publicly-available Level 2 or Level 3 charging stations

### Building Decarbonization

**Whole-Home Decarbonization Incentive:**
- **Rebate Amount:** Up to $12,000 per household
- **Purpose:** Cover remaining gaps after existing state and federal programs

**Illinois Climate Bank Financing:**
- Low-cost, easy-to-access finance offering for residential and small commercial decarbonization
- Available at point of sale
- Bridge loan to cover project costs until repaid by rebates/grants
- Remaining costs covered over 5-10 year period

**Building Codes (Implementation by July 1, 2025):**
- Statewide building codes under CDB (Public Act 103-510)
- Illinois Stretch Energy Code development (CEJA mandate)

### Workforce Development

**Grant Programs:**
- College of Lake County received CEJA grant for clean energy workforce pipeline
- Training areas: EV automotive technology, HVAC, energy efficiency audits, building and home inspection

**Progress Monitoring:**
- Joint study every 5 years (starting 2025) by IEPA, IPA, and ICC on:
  - Progress toward renewable energy goals
  - Electric resource adequacy and reliability

## 5. Heat Pump Focus

### Cold Climate Heat Pump Requirements

**General Requirements:**
- **Illinois Standard:** ENERGY STAR-rated cold climate heat pumps ONLY
- **Technology:** Modern cold climate heat pumps can effectively heat Midwestern homes even in extreme cold
- **Northern US Requirement:** Different requirements for ducted vs. ductless heat pumps

**Minimum Efficiency Standards (Example from Corn Belt Energy):**
- **Cooling Efficiency:** ≥15.2 SEER2
- **Heating Efficiency:** ≥7.5 HSPF2
- **Rebate:** $750

### Dual-Fuel Systems

**What is Dual-Fuel:**
- Combines electric air source heat pump with traditional gas furnace
- System switches between electricity and gas based on temperature

**Benefits:**
1. **Energy Efficiency:** Uses most efficient fuel source based on outdoor temperature
2. **Cost Savings:** Leverages most cost-effective fuel at any given time
3. **Reliability:** Consistent heating even in extreme cold weather
4. **Comfort:** Maintains comfortable indoor temperature year-round

**Eligible for Same Rebates:**
- Dual-fuel heat pumps qualify for the same rebates as heat pump-only systems
- Can stack utility rebates with federal tax credits

### Contractor Training Requirements

#### ComEd Heat Pump Training (Mandatory Starting January 1, 2025)

**30% Workforce Requirement:**
- 30% of installers and service technicians must complete heat pump training
- Required to participate in ComEd Home Heating and Cooling program and offer incentives

**Training Components:**

1. **Four Online On-Demand Modules**
   - Available through ComEd Customer Innovation Team
   - Self-paced learning

2. **Technical Training with Manufacturers**
   - Typically in-person, hands-on training
   - Covers equipment-specific installation and service

3. **Certification Process**
   - Contractors certify 30% workforce completion
   - Listed on ComEd trained heat pump contractor list
   - Enables access to product incentives

**Training Resources:**
- **Provider:** Slipstream (ComEd Air Source Heat Pumps Training)
- **Formats:** Webinars, lunch-and-learns, in-person training
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Website:** slipstreaminc.org/ComEd-ASHP

#### Ameren Illinois Contractor Requirements

**Flexible Contractor Approach:**
- NOT required to use approved contractor
- Ameren maintains database of preferred installers
- Homeowner can choose any licensed contractor

**Contractor Qualifications:**
- Licensed HVAC contractor
- Insurance required
- Familiar with Instant Incentive Distributor network

### Heat Pump Water Heaters

**Rebate Amounts:**
- **Ameren Illinois:** $1,150 instant discount
- **Federal Tax Credit:** Up to $2,000 (30% of cost)

**Stacking:**
- Can combine utility rebate + federal tax credit
- Maximum value: $3,150+ depending on installation cost

## 6. Federal Tax Credits

### Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (25C)

**CRITICAL DEADLINE:** December 31, 2025
**Recent Change:** End date moved up; originally expected to last into 2030s

#### Air Source Heat Pumps (Central and Mini-Split)

- **Credit Amount:** 30% of installation costs, capped at $2,000
- **Eligibility Date:** System must be installed and placed in service by December 31, 2025
- **Cold Climate Pathway:** Starting January 1, 2025, ENERGY STAR Most Efficient heat pumps eligible
- **Two Pathways:** One designed specifically for heating-dominated applications (cold climates)

**Minimum Efficiency Requirements:**
- **Cooling:** ≥15.2 SEER2
- **Heating:** ≥7.5 HSPF2

#### Heat Pump Water Heaters

- **Credit Amount:** 30% of cost, capped at $2,000
- **Same Deadline:** December 31, 2025
- **Combined Cap:** $2,000 annual limit even if installing multiple qualifying units (e.g., heat pump + water heater)

#### Important Notes

- **Tax Credit (Not Rebate):** Can only claim as much as owed in federal taxes for the year
- **No Retroactive Claims:** Must be installed in tax year claimed
- **Documentation Required:** AHRI certificate, manufacturer certification, proof of installation

## 7. Income-Qualified Programs

### Illinois HEEHRA/HEAR Program (Pending Launch)

**Status:** Application submitted to U.S. DOE in Summer 2024, awaiting approval
**Expected Launch:** Late 2025/Early 2026 (pilot program)

#### Program Overview

**Two Programs Under Inflation Reduction Act:**
1. **HOMES (Section 50121):** Home Energy Performance-Based, Whole House Rebates
2. **HEEHRA (Section 50122):** High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate Program

**Administering Agency:** Illinois EPA Office of Energy

#### Income Qualifications (Modified Proposal)

**Original Structure:**
- 80% AMI or less: 100% of project cost (up to $14,000 cap)
- 81-150% AMI: 50% of project cost

**Illinois Proposed Changes:**
- **Low-income households ONLY:** ≤80% Area Median Income (AMI)
- Adjusted funding distribution based on demand and interest
- Future expansion to moderate-income possible

#### Rebate Amounts

| Improvement Type | Maximum Rebate |
|------------------|----------------|
| Electric Heat Pump (space heating/cooling) | $8,000 |
| Electric Heat Pump Water Heater | $1,750 |
| Electric Stove/Cooktop/Range | $840 |
| Electric Clothes Dryer | $840 |
| Electric Load Service Center Upgrade | $4,000 |
| Insulation, Air Sealing, Ventilation | $1,600 |
| Contractor Installation Rebate | $500 |
| **TOTAL MAXIMUM** | **$14,000** |

**Flexibility:** Mix and match from different categories until hitting $14,000 cap

#### Important Program Rules

**No Retroactive Rebates:**
- Illinois EPA NOT planning to issue retroactive rebates
- Installation must occur after program launch

**Mutual Exclusivity:**
- Can receive rebate from EITHER HEEHRA OR HOMES program (not both)
- CAN combine rebate with energy efficiency tax credit

**Contractor Certification System:**
- Qualifying and certification system to be established
- May involve training programs
- Verification of experience with high-efficiency electric appliances and heating systems

#### Current Status and Next Steps

1. **Public Workshop:** Expected in 2025 (required for DOE approval)
2. **DOE Approval:** Awaiting federal approval
3. **Program Launch:** Pilot program late 2025/early 2026
4. **Full Launch:** TBD based on pilot results and funding

**Stay Updated:**
- **Website:** epa.illinois.gov/topics/energy/energy-rebates.html
- **Email Updates:** Sign up for Home Energy Rebate Updates

### Illinois Home Weatherization Assistance Program (IHWAP)

**Partnership:** IHWAP + Ameren Illinois

**Services Covered:**
- Air sealing
- Attic and wall insulation
- HVAC repair or replacement
- Water heater repair or replacement
- Electric base load reduction (lighting, refrigerator)
- Ventilation and moisture control
- Health and safety upgrades

**Maximum Benefit (LIHEAP FY 2025):**
- **Weatherization:** $11,000

**Eligibility:** Low-income residents and households

### Home Energy Savings (HES) Program

**Joint Initiative:** ComEd, Nicor Gas, Peoples Gas, North Shore Gas

**Services:**
1. **Direct-Install Measures:** Free energy-saving products
2. **Deep Retrofits:** Comprehensive upgrades
3. **Energy Advisor:** Paired assessment and personalized plan
4. **Referrals:** To IHWAP or utility-funded retrofits

**Income-Eligible Benefits:**
- No-cost weatherization, HVAC upgrades, health and safety measures
- Free advanced power strips
- Free smart thermostats
- Free LED bulbs and weather stripping

## 8. Contractor Requirements and Training

### ComEd Trade Ally Requirements

**To Participate in ComEd Programs:**

1. **Register as ComEd Residential Trade Ally**
   - Complete online registration
   - Provide licensing and insurance documentation

2. **Complete Heat Pump Training (For Heat Pump Rebates)**
   - Four online on-demand modules
   - Manufacturer technical training (in-person)
   - Certify 30% of workforce completion

3. **Maintain Eligibility**
   - Keep 30% of installers/technicians trained
   - Update training as requirements evolve
   - Listed on ComEd trained heat pump contractor website

**Customer Access:**
- Customers MUST use ComEd Residential Trade Ally to qualify for rebates
- Trade Ally list available on ComEd website

### Ameren Illinois Contractor Flexibility

**No Mandatory Contractor List:**
- Customers NOT required to use approved contractor
- Any licensed HVAC contractor can install equipment

**Preferred Installer Database:**
- Ameren maintains database of contractors familiar with program
- Not mandatory but recommended

**Instant Incentive Distributor Network:**
- Contractors should establish relationships with participating distributors
- Enables upfront discount pass-through to customers

### EV Charging Installation Requirements

**For CEJA EV Charging Grants:**
- **Licensed Electrician Required**
- **Compliance:** Health and safety codes, architectural standards
- **Insurance:** Certificate of insurance mandatory
- **Documentation:** Detailed project documentation for grant application

### General Best Practices

1. **Stay Informed**
   - Subscribe to utility program updates
   - Monitor CEJA implementation
   - Track federal tax credit changes

2. **Customer Education**
   - Explain stacking opportunities (utility + federal + state)
   - Clarify income-qualified options
   - Provide timeline expectations (especially HEEHRA delays)

3. **Documentation**
   - Maintain AHRI certificates
   - Keep manufacturer certifications
   - Photograph installations
   - Provide detailed invoices with equipment specifications

4. **Equipment Specifications**
   - Verify ENERGY STAR certification
   - Confirm cold climate rating (Illinois requirement)
   - Meet or exceed minimum efficiency thresholds
   - Check AHRI directory for certified combinations
